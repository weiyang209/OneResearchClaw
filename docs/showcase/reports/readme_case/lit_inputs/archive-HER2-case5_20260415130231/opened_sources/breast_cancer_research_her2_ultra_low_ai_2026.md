# AI-enhanced precision: boosting pathologist confidence in HER2 ultra-low diagnosis through intelligent re-screening

**Source:** Breast Cancer Research, 2026
**URL:** https://breast-cancer-research.biomedcentral.com/article/10.1186/s13058-026-02261-4
**Access date:** 2026-04-15

## Abstract

This study evaluates an AI tool (Roche Navify Digital Pathology platform) for assisting pathologists in diagnosing HER2 ultra-low expression in breast cancer. Among 92 HER2 ultra-low slides (40 CNB + 52 surgical excision), AI assistance significantly improved pathologists' detection performance against expert consensus ground truth: F1-score increased from 0.78 to 0.98, precision from 0.39 to 0.91, recall from 0.72 to 0.96.

## Key Results

### HER2 Ultra-Low Detection Improvement
| Metric | Without AI | With AI |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| F1-score | 0.78 | 0.98 |
| Precision | 0.39 | 0.91 |
| Recall | 0.72 | 0.96 |

### Concordance Improvement
| Setting | Without AI | With AI |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| CNB-SE concordance (non-NAT) | 0.37 | 0.48 |
| Post-NAT specimen consistency | 0.63 | 0.92 |

## Clinical Significance

1. **HER2 ultra-low** (~10% of breast cancers) is critical for ADC therapy eligibility (trastuzumab deruxtecan)
2. AI assistance primarily reduces HER2 underestimation
3. AI significantly elevates diagnostic agreement among pathologists
4. Reduces interpretation time and increases high-confidence assessments

## Dataset

- 188 breast cancer cases (376 whole-slide images)
- 136 matched CNB-surgical excision pairs (no neoadjuvant therapy)
- 52 matched CNB-post-NAT pairs (HER2 IHC scores 0 or 1+)
- External validation cohort: 89 slides from 2 independent hospitals
- Scanned with Roche VENTANA DP 600 scanner

## Limitations

- External validation on only 89 slides from 2 hospitals
- AI tool is commercial (Roche Navify) - not openly available
- Study conducted by institution that also developed the AI tool

## Relevance

This paper provides strong evidence that AI assistance dramatically improves HER2-low/ultra-low detection, directly addressing the clinical applicability challenge noted in the BCI dataset paper (pathologist accuracy 37.5-40% on generated images).
