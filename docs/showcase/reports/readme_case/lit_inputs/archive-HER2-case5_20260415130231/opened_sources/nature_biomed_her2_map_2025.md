# Deep-learning-based HER2 status assessment from multimodal breast cancer data predicts neoadjuvant therapy response

**Source:** Nature Biomedical Engineering, 2025
**URL:** https://nature.com/articles/s41551-025-01495-5
**Access date:** 2026-04-15

## Abstract

Accurate assessment of human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2) status is crucial for effective breast cancer treatment planning. This paper introduces the deep-learning-based HER2 multimodal alignment and prediction (MAP) model, which leverages pretreatment multimodal breast cancer images for comprehensive tumour characterisation and HER2 status prediction. The model was trained on a large-scale dataset of 14,472 images from 6,991 cases across 4 medical centres. The results demonstrate superiority over traditional needle biopsies. Code available at https://github.com/ZhangJD-ong/HER2-MAP-from-Multimodal-Breast-Data.

## Key Contributions

1. HER2 MAP model integrates multimodal breast cancer images to predict HER2 status and neoadjuvant therapy response
2. Large-scale dataset: 14,472 images from 6,991 cases across 4 medical centres
3. Addresses intratumoural heterogeneity through comprehensive multimodal analysis
4. Provides more accurate HER2 prediction than traditional needle biopsies

## Clinical Significance

- Addresses limitations of traditional needle biopsies which are limited in tissue sampling and often inaccurate due to intratumoural heterogeneity
- Multi-modal imaging provides more comprehensive tumour characterisation
- Supports informed clinical decisions and treatment plans

## Dataset

- Multimodal datasets from 4 medical centres
- Centre C data available at TCIA (Cancer Imaging Archive)
- In-house raw data available upon reasonable academic request

## Limitations

- Reliance on multimodal imaging data which may not always be available
- Model performance depends on image quality and preprocessing
